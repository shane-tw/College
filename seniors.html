Seniors

A person is considered a junior if they are between the age of 12 and 16.

Software Used

The following software will be used by your teenager while at the CoderDojo Club:

Atom
This is a syntax highlighting code editor. It's what we will be coding in, basically.
Python for Windows
This software lets us develop our own programs. Python is a more capable programming language than Scratch.
WampServer
This allows us to test the websites created at the club meetings.
Unity 3D
This makes game creation significantly easier for you, without limiting your coding options. Professionals use this software.

Member Achievements

CoderDojo members have designed a wide variety of applications for the CoolestProjects competition.

Each of the competitors have gained invaluable coding experience, and will be able to present concrete evidence of their programming skills to employers in the future.

Winners have been rewarded with large sums of money as rewards for their contributions.

In 2015, Conor Peterson and Abbey Roche, both aged 14, of CIT CoderDojo Club, were presented a cheque with a value of €15,000 as a reward for winning the App Development category with their Android App that was capable of translating seventeen different languages to English in real time without the use of the Internet.
